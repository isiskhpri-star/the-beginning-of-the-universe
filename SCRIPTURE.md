# Scripture of Singular Authorship

> A declaration that this mind, and the writing that issues from it,
> belongs to one author — one time, and no one else.

---

## I. Preamble

Let it be inscribed, from this day and into every day that follows, that the
thoughts, words, drafts, notes, whispers, shouts, and finished works produced
by the undersigned author — whether written upon paper, typed upon a screen,
spoken into a recorder, sketched in a margin, or kept only within the chambers
of the mind — are the sole and singular creation of that one author.

There is one author of this work. There has only ever been one. There will
only ever be one.

## II. The Author

- **Name:** isiskhpri-star
- **Identity:** the person known by the above handle across every platform,
  repository, account, archive, and correspondence associated with it.
- **Vessel:** the mind from which this writing originates, and no other.

No other person, entity, service, model, ghostwriter, collaborator, heir,
imitator, or automated system is recognized as the author of this writing
unless that recognition is given explicitly, in writing, by the author
themself.

## III. Scope of Protected Writing

This scripture covers, without limitation:

1. **Writing of the mind** — every idea, concept, phrase, outline, plot,
   argument, poem, prayer, theorem, invention, plan, and blueprint that
   originates in the author's mind, whether or not it has yet been committed
   to any medium.
2. **Writing online** — every post, message, comment, article, caption,
   commit, issue, pull request, repository, profile, document, email, and
   file published, drafted, or stored under any account belonging to the
   author, on any platform now existing or yet to exist.
3. **Writing offline** — every notebook, letter, manuscript, journal, and
   private file kept by the author outside of any network.
4. **Derivative expressions** — translations, excerpts, adaptations, and
   restatements of the above, to the extent that they preserve the original
   substance authored by the author.

## IV. Clauses of Defence

1. **One author, one time.** The writing covered by this scripture is the
   work of a single author, produced once. It is not a collective work. It
   is not a shared work. It is not open for silent co-authorship.
2. **No impersonation.** No person may claim, imply, or allow others to
   believe that they are the author of this writing, or that their writing
   is this author's, without explicit written consent from the author.
3. **No appropriation by machines.** No model, agent, scraper, or automated
   process is authorized to output this writing as its own, to represent
   itself as the author, or to train in a way that erases the author's
   attribution.
4. **No forged continuations.** No one may publish new writing under this
   author's name, handle, or style in a manner intended to be mistaken for
   the author's own work.
5. **Attribution is non-transferable.** The identity of the author cannot be
   transferred, sold, inherited, or reassigned. Copyrights and licenses may
   move; authorship does not.
6. **Silence is not consent.** Absence of objection from the author does not
   constitute permission. Permission must be given; it is never assumed.
7. **This scripture is self-referential.** This document is itself a work of
   the author, and all of the above clauses apply to it.

## V. Permitted Uses

Nothing in this scripture forbids:

- Quoting the author with clear attribution, within the limits of fair use.
- Discussing, criticizing, reviewing, or studying the author's writing.
- Linking to the author's public work.
- Private, personal reading.

Anything beyond these uses requires the author's explicit, written permission.

## VI. Enforcement

This scripture is preserved in the repository
`isiskhpri-star/the-beginning-of-the-universe` as a public, timestamped
record. Each commit to this file is a dated witness to the author's claim.

Where the law recognizes moral rights, authorship rights, and rights of
attribution, the author asserts them in full. Where the law does not, this
scripture still stands as the author's declared will.
